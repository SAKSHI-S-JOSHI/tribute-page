Sir M. Visvesvaraya

1861-1962

Father of Engineering

Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was a civil engineer, statesman and the 19th Diwan of Mysore. He was responsible for significant engineering projects in India, including the Krishna Raja Sagara dam in Mandya, which helped convert nearby wasteland into fertile land and became the primary source of drinking water for many neighbouring cities. The dam itself continues to draw thousands of visitors every year.  Beyond his astonishing achievements in engineering, Sir M. Visvesvaraya is credited for his extraordinary work in accelerating industrialisation and education in India. He was appointed a Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire (CIE) in recognition of his achievements and contribution to Indian society.

Fascinating facts

  • Visvesvaraya founded the Government and Engineering College, which was later re-named University Visvesvaraya College of Engineering (UVCE) in his honour.
  • He received the Indian Republic's highest honour, the Bharat Ratna, in 1955.
  • Every year, his birthday marks the celebration of Engineer’s Day in India.
  • He was knighted as Knight Commander of the British Indian Empire (KCIE) by King George V for his contribution to public good.
  • On his 100th Birthday, 15 September 1960, India Post issued a postage stamp of Visvesvaraya.
